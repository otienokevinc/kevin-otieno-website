Advisory Philosophy

How I think about finance.

Three convictions, formed over eight years of pricing risk, managing capital, and building the models decision-makers actually rely on.

01

Every model tells a story.

A sensitivity table is not just math; it is a map of futures. A DSCR schedule is not just a compliance check; it is a stress test of conviction. The best financial models don't merely compute; they communicate. I build models that decision-makers can read, challenge, and trust, not black boxes that ask for faith.

02

Risk is information.

From pricing P&C risk in Nairobi to stress-testing IRR scenarios for an East African eco-resort development, I have spent eight years treating uncertainty not as a threat to manage but as a signal to decode. Downside scenarios and covenant pressure are the places where real strategy is revealed, not the base case.

03

Precision is respect.

Whether presenting to an eight-member investment committee or advising a first-time entrepreneur, the people I work with deserve rigorous, well-built financial tools, not shortcuts. Getting the terminal growth rate right matters. Getting the WACC right matters. I do not cut corners on the things that matter.

HOW THIS SHOWS UP IN PRACTICE

Standards I hold across every engagement.

→

Downside cases are mandatory

Every valuation or project-finance model I deliver includes a bear case and a stress test, not just a base case dressed up with a sensitivity tab.

→

Assumptions are shown, not buried

WACC derivations, terminal growth logic, and DSCR covenant thresholds are visible and defensible, not hidden in a locked cell.

→

Results are attribution-checked

Performance claims are decomposed: Brinson-Hood-Beebower attribution on portfolio returns, cohort analysis on underwriting pricing; the result can be explained, not just reported.

→

The audience shapes the delivery

A board gets a different document than an investment committee, which gets a different document than a first-time entrepreneur, same rigor, different translation.
