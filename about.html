About

Over a decade at the intersection of risk, capital, and judgment.

I spend most of my time answering one question for the people I work with: can this number actually be trusted, and what should you do because of it. That's shown up as pricing insurance risk in Nairobi, managing a live equity portfolio in Kentucky, and building the models that told a sponsor their construction budget was already short before the first shovel went in the ground. Whatever the room, the job is the same: turn uncertain, incomplete information into a decision someone can stand behind.

BACKGROUND

The short version.

Finance, investment, and risk professional with an MSc in Finance (University of Kentucky, Gatton College of Business), a Bachelor of Commerce in Actuarial Science (Kenyatta University), and current CFA Level I candidacy. Over a decade of experience across finance, risk, and analytics, including eight years in professional roles spanning commercial insurance underwriting and brokerage, active equity portfolio management, corporate FP&A, and independent financial-strategy consulting, spanning Kenya, the United States, and remote engagements globally. I hold the FMVA® (Financial Modeling & Valuation Analyst) charter from the Corporate Finance Institute, and I currently serve as a Senior Domain Expert evaluating AI-generated financial outputs for Mercor.io, alongside expert-consulting work for GLG and Catalant.

What sets this combination apart is range: quantitative modeling (Python, SQL, DCF and LBO construction, sensitivity engines) paired with qualitative, client-facing judgment built across underwriting, brokerage, and board advisory. Few practitioners carry both the modeling depth and the commercial instinct to know which number actually matters to the person making the decision.

WHAT I BRING TO A TEAM

Beyond the modeling work.

01

I've sat on both sides of the table

Underwriting taught me how a lender or insurer actually thinks about risk. Advisory work taught me how a founder or investment committee actually hears a number. I build for the audience in the room, not just the model on the screen.

02

I don't wait to be asked the hard question

The eco-resort model I built surfaced a construction budget gap and a debt coverage shortfall before the sponsor's own team caught either one. I'd rather find the problem in the spreadsheet than have someone else find it in a covenant breach.

03

I carry a book of business, not just a body of work

Zero attrition across a 130-account portfolio wasn't luck. It came from showing up consistently for renewal conversations most brokers treat as routine. That same instinct for the relationship behind the number shows up in every advisory engagement I take on.

04

I put my own work behind my name

Published research, sourced case studies, and a track record that traces back to an actual model or account, not a claim. If you're deciding whether to bring someone in, that's the kind of paper trail worth checking.
