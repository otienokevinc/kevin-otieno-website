About

Eight years at the intersection of risk, capital, and judgment.

From pricing non-life insurance risk in Nairobi to managing a live $6M equity portfolio in Kentucky, my career has stayed close to one question: what does the data actually say, and what should we do about it.

BACKGROUND

The short version.

Finance, investment, and risk professional with an MSc in Finance (University of Kentucky, Gatton College of Business), a Bachelor of Commerce in Actuarial Science (Kenyatta University), and current CFA Level I candidacy. My work spans commercial insurance underwriting and brokerage, active equity portfolio management, corporate FP&A, and independent financial-strategy consulting, spanning Kenya, the United States, and remote engagements globally. I hold the FMVA® (Financial Modeling & Valuation Analyst) charter from the Corporate Finance Institute, and I currently serve as a Senior Domain Expert evaluating AI-generated financial outputs for Mercor.io, alongside expert-consulting work for GLG and Catalant.

What sets this combination apart is range: quantitative modeling (Python, SQL, DCF and LBO construction, sensitivity engines) paired with qualitative, client-facing judgment built across underwriting, brokerage, and board advisory. Few practitioners carry both the modeling depth and the commercial instinct to know which number actually matters to the person making the decision.

MISSION

Decision-ready analysis, not decoration.

To give investors, boards, and operators financial models and analysis rigorous enough to bear real capital decisions, translated clearly enough that no one in the room has to take the number on faith.

VISION

Rigor as a standard, not a differentiator.

A practice where institutional-grade modeling discipline, stress-tested assumptions, attribution-checked results, is simply how financial advisory is done, for a first-time entrepreneur and an investment committee alike.
